# BPlayer Privacy Policy

_Last updated: 2026-05-10_

BPlayer is a local audio player. It does not collect, store, transmit, or share
any personal data.

## What BPlayer accesses on your device

- **A folder you choose.** When you first open BPlayer, you pick a folder via
  the Android system folder picker (Storage Access Framework). BPlayer reads
  audio files and folder structure from inside that folder so it can list and
  play them. It does not access anything outside the folder you chose.
- **Playback position.** BPlayer stores, on your device only, where you stopped
  in each book/folder so it can resume from the same point next time. This
  information never leaves your device.

## What BPlayer does NOT do

- BPlayer does not collect personal information.
- BPlayer does not contain any analytics, tracking, advertising, or third-party
  SDKs that send data off-device.
- BPlayer does not make network requests. It has no internet permission.
- BPlayer does not use accounts, login, or sign-in.

## Permissions BPlayer requests

- **Foreground service / media playback.** Required by Android to play audio
  with the screen off without being killed by the system.
- **Post notifications.** Required by Android 13+ to show the playback
  notification on the lock screen.
- **Wake lock.** Keeps the CPU awake while a track is playing.

None of these are used to collect or transmit data.

## Children

BPlayer plays whatever audio files you put in the folder you chose. It does
not collect data about children, or about anyone.

## Changes

If this policy ever changes, the updated text will be posted at the same URL
as this document. There is no email list, account, or other channel through
which we could notify you, because we have none of those.
