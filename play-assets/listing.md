## Full description (≤ 4000 chars)

```
BPlayer is a small, focused audio player for people who keep their books and
music as folders of files.

Pick a root folder once. BPlayer shows your folders as a clean list and plays
whatever's inside — chapter-by-chapter audiobooks, single .m4b files, music
albums. No library scanning, no metadata madness, no account.

Every book remembers where you stopped — the file you were on and the position
inside it. Switch from Book A to Book B, listen for an hour, come back to Book
A: it picks up where you left off. Each book has its own bookmark, kept on
your device.

WHAT'S INSIDE
• Folder browser — tap to drill in or play.
• Resume per book — switch between books freely.
• Android Auto — same browse tree shows up in your car.
• Now Playing screen — large cover, draggable scrubber.
• Pull-to-refresh — picks up files added by Syncthing or any sync tool.
• Per-row delete and reset-progress.
• Cover art picked from Cover.jpg / Front.jpg / first jpg in the folder.
• No ads, no analytics, no network.

REQUIRES
• Android 14 or newer.
• Audio files (mp3, m4a, m4b, flac, ogg, opus, wav, aac) on your phone.

ANDROID AUTO
Sideloaded media apps don't appear in Android Auto by default. To enable:
in the Android Auto app, tap Version 10 times to enable developer mode, then
turn on Unknown sources. BPlayer will then show up in the car's media app
picker.

PRIVACY
BPlayer doesn't collect data. It has no internet permission. The only thing
stored on your device is the playback position for each folder. See the
privacy policy for details.

OPEN SOURCE
Source code at https://github.com/skranin/bplayer (MIT license).
```
